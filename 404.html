Is Ryan a good programmer?

Let G mean “Ryan is a good programmer,” and E the evidence (you opened this 404). Bayes’ rule maps your prior on G to a posterior after E:

P(G | E) = P(E | G) · P(G) / P(E)
with P(E) = P(E | G)P(G) + P(E | ¬G)(1 − P(G))

Each slider is 0 to 100%. The posterior and P(E) update as you move them.
